“A Rainbow for a Reason” – An Open Conversation with Lucy and Mel

Lucy admits that as a trans woman, she still would not feel comfortable in the office being her authentic self on her first day in a new role. Is that the organization we want to be? How can we help?

31st March 2021 is Trans Day of Visibility and to help us understand some of the not so obvious issues and prejudices faced by trans people both at work and in a wider context, HS2's Onboard Network interviewed Lucy Gilbert, Software Engineer from Trail App and her mum, Melodie Gilbert, Occupational Hygienist from Park Health and Safety Partnership who are working with Phase 1's Fusion in delivering HS2.

HS2's Dan Smith had an open conversation with Lucy and Mel asking them questions that show how being a trans woman and having a trans daughter can provide some pretty big life hurdles; hurdles that cisgender people (people whose sex matches their gender) may never have considered.

Watch this short video where Lucy and Mel give their view on some of the issues around transitioning and how workplaces can be more trans inclusive.

Being an ally to trans people not only helps overcome some of these hurdles but also promotes diversity within teams and businesses. Cherishing diversity in teams harnesses the wider benefits of diverse thinking, bringing with it a breadth of experience and talent.
